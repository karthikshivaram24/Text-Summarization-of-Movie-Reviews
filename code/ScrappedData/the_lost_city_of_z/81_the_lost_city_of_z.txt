Historians have long been fascinated by Percy Fawcett, the British explorer who, in 1925, went off on the last of his journeys to the Amazon jungle, never to be heard from again. Fawcett had been searching for the City of Z, as he called it, the nexus of an ancient but advanced civilization whose traces he believed he had found in previous expeditions. Those exploits have been brought to the screen by James Gray in “The Lost City of Z,” which stars Charlie Hunnam as Fawcett and was adapted from the David Grann nonfiction book of the same name.
