★★☆☆☆ The term “epic boy’s own adventure” has been much used about James Gray’s new film, and it is exactly that: the story of a man who never quite grew up, and never quite succeeded in his mission, celebrated in two and a half grandiose but curiously flat hours of jungletastic screen time. It stars Charlie Hunnam and his natty waxed moustache as Lieutenant-Colonel Percy Fawcett, a British military chap sent out by the Royal Geographical Society in 1906 to map the unknown Rio Verde, a crucial river border between Bolivia and Brazil, during land wars over rubber (“black gold”).
